Machu Picchu

The Lost Sanctuary in the Clouds

1 The Inca Masterpiece

Built in the 15th century (around 1450 AD) by the Inca Emperor Pachacuti, Machu Picchu sits 7,970 feet above sea level in the Andes Mountains of Peru. It was likely a royal estate or a sacred religious site for Inca leaders.

2 Earthquake-Proof Engineering

The Incas used a technique called "Ashlar masonry," where stones are cut so precisely that they fit together without any mortar (cement). This makes the buildings earthquake-proof; during a tremor, the stones "dance" and then fall back perfectly into place.
