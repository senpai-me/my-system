Chichen Itza

The Sacred Mayan City of Serpents

1 The Mayan Metropolis

Located in Mexico’s Yucatan Peninsula, Chichen Itza was one of the largest and most powerful Mayan cities between 600-1200 AD. The name means "At the mouth of the well of the Itza," referring to the sacred natural sinkholes (Cenotes) found nearby.

2 El Castillo: The Giant Calendar

The main pyramid, El Castillo, is actually a massive stone calendar. It has 4 stairways with 91 steps each, plus the top platform, totaling **365 steps**—one for every day of the year.

3 The Serpent Shadow Illusion

Twice a year (Equinox), the sun creates a shadow that looks like a giant serpent sliding down the pyramid’s stairs. This was the Mayans' way of showing the feathered serpent god, Kukulcan, descending to Earth.

4 The Great Ball Court

Chichen Itza has the largest ancient ball court in the Americas. Players had to hit a heavy rubber ball through high stone hoops without using their hands. Legend says the game was so serious that sometimes the losers (or winners) were sacrificed to the gods.

5 Advanced Astronomy

The Mayans were master astronomers. They built "El Caracol," an observatory with windows aligned to the movements of Venus and the stars, helping them create the most accurate calendars in the ancient world.

6 New World Wonder

After being abandoned in the 1400s and swallowed by the jungle, it was rediscovered and eventually named one of the New Seven Wonders of the World in 2007. It remains a masterpiece of ancient architecture and science.
