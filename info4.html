The Colosseum

The Grand Arena of Gladiators & Empire

1 The Flavian Amphitheatre

Built between 70-80 AD under Emperor Vespasian and his son Titus, it is the largest ancient amphitheatre ever built. It was a gift to the Roman people, constructed on the site of a former lake from Emperor Nero's palace.

2 Massive Capacity

The arena could hold an estimated 50,000 to 80,000 spectators. It had 80 entrances, allowing the massive crowd to enter and exit in just minutesâ€”a design so efficient that modern stadiums still use it today.

3 The Hypogeum (Underground)

Beneath the arena floor was a complex network of tunnels and cages called the Hypogeum. Here, gladiators and wild animals (lions, tigers, bears) were kept before being lifted by manual elevators to surprise the audience.

4 Mock Naval Battles

In its early years, the Colosseum could actually be flooded with water! They used complex plumbing to fill the arena and stage "Naumachia" or mock sea battles with real ships for the entertainment of the public.
