Petra

The Half-Built, Half-Carved Magic in Stone

1 The Nabataean Capital

Located in modern-day Jordan, Petra was the thriving capital of the Nabataean Empire around 400 BC. These people were master traders who grew incredibly wealthy by controlling the spice and incense routes of the ancient world.

2 Carved, Not Built

Unlike most cities made of bricks, Petra is famous because its massive temples and tombs were carved directly into pink sandstone cliffs. The most famous structure, Al-Khazneh (The Treasury), was likely a tomb for a King.

3 Masters of Water

Petra is located in the middle of a desert, yet it had lush gardens and pools. The Nabataeans were genius engineers who built a complex system of clay pipes and dams to capture every drop of rainwater, turning the desert into an oasis.

4 Hidden for Centuries

After several massive earthquakes and changes in trade routes, the city was abandoned and forgotten by the Western world for hundreds of years. It was only "rediscovered" in 1812 by a Swiss explorer who disguised himself as an Arab scholar to sneak in.

5 The Entrance: The Siq

To enter Petra, you have to walk through a narrow, winding canyon called the "Siq." It’s over 1km long with walls 80 meters high. At the end of this dark path, the sunlit Treasury suddenly appears—one of the most dramatic views on Earth.

6 Hollywood & World Wonder

Petra became a global icon after being featured in movies like *Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade*. In 2007, it was voted one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, recognized for its incredible fusion of nature and architecture.
